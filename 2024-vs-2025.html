Key Insights

What the data tells us about 2024 vs 2025 performance

Critical Finding: Ontario & New York Collapse

Our two largest markets - New York (-$299K, -16%) and Ontario (-$342K, -22%) - account for $641K of lost revenue. These markets alone represent 60% of the total YoY decline. Key accounts like Herald Wholesale (-$138K), Decor Planet Brooklyn (-$108K), and Canaroma (-$83K) are driving this collapse.

Bright Spot: Studio 41 Highland Park

Studio 41 Highland Park grew +11% ($455K to $506K) while almost every other top-10 account declined. They are now our #1 account by 2025 revenue. Worth investigating: What are they doing differently? Can we replicate their success with other dealers?

Emerging Growth: Colorado, Virginia, Nevada

Secondary markets are showing strength: Colorado (+$67K, +13%), Virginia (+$65K, +64%), and Nevada (+$60K, +32%). Combined growth of $192K. These markets are smaller but growing - potential focus areas for expansion.

Question for VP Sales Context
1. Why did Herald Wholesale drop 49%? $280K to $142K - our 4th largest account in 2024. Lost a competitor? Personnel change?
2. What happened in Arizona? State dropped 48% ($300K to $157K). Central Arizona Supply and Studio 41 Scottsdale both down significantly.
3. Can we replicate Studio 41's success? Only major account growing (+11%). What training, incentives, or support do they have that others don't?
4. Should we double down on Colorado? Rampart Denver steady, state growing 13%. Potential for more dealer recruitment?
